MSc Thesis
TU Delft
October 2020 – April 2022 Delft, Netherlands

Study of the dynamics of a brush for use in active debris removal (link)

Space debris has the potential to damage space assets in the future. This project focuses on developing a numerical model to study the dynamics of a brush. This model can be used to study how a brush employs soft contact to detumble and capture tumbling space debris.


  • Studied different methods to model the dynamics of the flexible filaments of a brush
  • Developed a numerical model of a brush using Discrete Element Method to simulate its dynamic behaviour
  • Verified the behaviour of a bristle predicted by the model using the Cosserat rod model and hardware experiments
